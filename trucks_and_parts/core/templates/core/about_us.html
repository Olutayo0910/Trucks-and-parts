Sinotruk History

Since its inception in 1960, Sinotruk has been a pioneering force in China's heavy truck industry. The company's journey began with the production of China's first heavy truck, the Yellow River JN150 eight-ton truck, a monumental achievement that ended China's reliance on imported heavy trucks.

Throughout the 1960s and 1970s, Sinotruk continued to innovate, developing the Yellow River 350 tipper truck and establishing collaborations with international partners to assemble Roman trucks in SKD mode.

In the 1980s, Sinotruk embraced advanced technology by introducing Steyr heavy vehicle manufacturing technology from Austria. This marked the beginning of Sinotruk's localization efforts, culminating in the production of the first locally made Steyr truck in China in 1985.

The early 2000s saw Sinotruk's rapid growth and expansion, with the establishment of the largest heavy-duty truck axle production base in China in 2002. The launch of the Steyr King HDT and the signing of a contract with Volvo for a joint venture further solidified Sinotruk's position as a technological leader.

In 2004, Sinotruk made a significant leap forward with the launch of the HOWO heavy truck, which quickly became the best-selling model in China. This success was followed by the listing of Sinotruk on the main board of the Hong Kong Stock Exchange in 2007, signaling its entry into the global capital market.

Subsequent years saw Sinotruk's continuous innovation and expansion into new areas, including the launch of the HOWO A7, the introduction of light truck products, and the establishment of a strategic partnership with MAN.

In 2015, Sinotruk completed the construction of China's only "National Research Center for HDT Engineering and Technology," demonstrating its commitment to research and innovation. The introduction of smart trucks in 2016 and the trial operation of the world's first unmanned electric truck in 2018 further solidified Sinotruk's position as a leader in technological advancement.

Today, Sinotruk continues to push the boundaries of innovation with the launch of the new-generation HDT brand Yellow River, equipped with advanced features and intelligent driving capabilities. As Sinotruk enters a new era of development under new leadership, its legacy of excellence and commitment to customer satisfaction remains stronger than ever.
